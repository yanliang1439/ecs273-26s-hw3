National Bank of Greece is now working off a slightly higher internal fair value estimate, with the model price target moving from about €15.64 to roughly €15.92. That shift sits alongside Street targets that cluster in the €15.70 to €16.10 range, including JPMorgan’s €15.70 and €16.10 points, which gives you a sense of how closely aligned current pricing views have become. Reading on, you will see how these price target tweaks feed into the broader analyst narrative and what to watch as it evolves.

JPMorgan and Deutsche Bank sit in a tight band on valuation, with recent target points between €15.70 and €16.10, which broadly aligns with current internal fair value work around €15.90.
JPMorgan’s most recent move to a €16.10 price target and Deutsche Bank’s €15.95 level signal that both firms still see room for value at or slightly above the current cluster of targets.
JPMorgan previously reset its target from €16.40 to €15.70, which highlights more cautious assumptions around execution or growth, even while keeping a supportive rating in place.
Goldman Sachs has downgraded National Bank of Greece, introducing a more critical voice on the stock and reminding you that not all coverage is aligned with the higher target range cited by JPMorgan and Deutsche Bank.
