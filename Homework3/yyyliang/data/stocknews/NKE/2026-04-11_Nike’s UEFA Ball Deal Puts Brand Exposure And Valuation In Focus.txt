Nike is reportedly close to becoming the official match ball provider for the UEFA Champions League.
The deal would end Adidas's 25 year run as ball supplier for Europe's top club competition.
The reported agreement is also expected to cover the Europa League and Conference League.
Nike, traded as NYSE:NKE, is seeing this branding news arrive after a weak share price patch, with the stock at $42.62. The share price shows declines of 3.6% over the past week, 23.5% over 30 days, and 32.6% year to date, with longer term 3 year and 5 year returns also negative. This new UEFA exposure gives investors another factor to watch alongside recent share price pressure.
The potential Champions League ball deal could increase Nike's visibility with fans and broadcasters across Europe, which may be important for the company's football business over time. For investors, it adds a fresh branding development to monitor as part of the broader case for or against NYSE:NKE, alongside fundamentals, valuation and competitive dynamics.
