In recent days, analysts have repeatedly flagged ongoing competitive and pricing pressures on Pfizer’s key products, including Paxlovid, Vyndamax and Prevnar, along with softer visibility for its COVID-19 vaccine franchise and pipeline execution risks.
At the same time, Pfizer has been reshaping its portfolio through acquisitions, pipeline expansion and cost-saving efforts, creating a tension between product headwinds, dividend questions and its longer-term innovation ambitions.
We’ll now examine how these concerns over pricing pressure on Pfizer’s core medicines might reshape the existing investment narrative around its pipeline-driven recovery.

To own Pfizer today, you need to believe that its large late stage pipeline and recent acquisitions can offset pricing pressure on core drugs and a stretched dividend. The most important near term catalyst is upcoming Phase 3 and regulatory outcomes across oncology and vaccines, while the biggest current risk is sustained pricing and competitive pressure on Paxlovid, Vyndamax and Prevnar. The recent bearish analyst calls mainly reinforce this risk rather than changing it.
In that context, RBC Capital’s reiterated Sell rating and forecast 4% year over year revenue decline ahead of first quarter results is especially relevant. It crystallizes concerns that ongoing price erosion in Vyndamax and softer COVID demand could undermine confidence in Pfizer’s pipeline driven recovery, even as management points to oncology assets like sigvotatug vedotin and its Lyme vaccine as key potential offsets.
