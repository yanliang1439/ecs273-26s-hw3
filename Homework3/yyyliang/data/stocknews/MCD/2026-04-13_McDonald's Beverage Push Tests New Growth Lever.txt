McDonald's Corp. (NYSE:MCD) ispreparing to roll outenergy drinks and crafted sodas across its U.S. restaurants, with new items including a Red Bull Dragonberry Energizer and flavored sodas, with the cold drinks rolling out next month and energy drinks following in August. Shares werelittle changedin pre-market.
McDonald's plans to position these drinks below competitors such as Starbucks (NASDAQ:SBUX) and Dutch Bros (NYSE:BROS), targeting price-sensitive consumers while tapping into a fast-growing segment that has driven traffic for specialty chains. Beverages are among the highest-margin items in quick-service restaurants.
The launch follows the same affordability strategy that McDonald's is pursuing after the Under $3 Menu and $4 breakfast meal McDonald's rolled out earlier this month. CEO Chris Kempczinski said in February the value strategy was drawing more low-income visits, and beverages are the natural next lever.
The risk is operational. Expanding into more complex, customizable drinks could slow service times, particularly across franchise-heavy operations, even as the company tries to balance throughput with higher-margin mix.
