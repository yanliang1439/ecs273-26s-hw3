Amazon.com (NasdaqGS:AMZN) is partnering with Delta Air Lines to roll out satellite based in flight Wi Fi using Project Kuiper, marking Kuiper’s first major commercial deployment.
At the same time, Amazon is expanding its presence in India’s quick commerce space, adding more dark stores as competition and order volumes in the segment increase.
For investors watching NasdaqGS:AMZN, these moves sit outside the familiar core of e commerce and cloud services. In flight connectivity and ultra fast local delivery are both areas where customer expectations are rising. In these markets, large partners or dense local networks can matter as much as brand recognition.
The Delta agreement gives Project Kuiper a real world test bed with an established airline partner. India’s quick commerce push targets a market many global peers consider important for long term growth. Together, these initiatives add extra dimensions to how Amazon might be positioned across connectivity and local commerce over the coming years.
