Coca-Cola’s updated fair value estimate has shifted slightly, from US$83.49 to US$83.67 per share, putting a finer point on where analysts see the stock’s long term potential. This adjustment sits alongside a cluster of higher price targets in the high US$80s and around US$90, reflecting views on pricing power, category mix, and exposure to protein focused beverages. As you read on, you will see how these evolving targets feed into the broader analyst narrative and what to watch next in that story.

Morgan Stanley has named Coca-Cola its Top Pick in beverages and its Top Pick overall in North American consumer staples, highlighting confidence in the company’s execution and category positioning.
BofA, Jefferies and Morgan Stanley have all lifted their Coca-Cola price targets into the high US$80s to around US$90, signaling that several large firms see room in the valuation for the company’s current plan.
Jefferies points to exposure to protein focused beverages and other "easy protein" formats as a positive, grouping Coca-Cola with BellRing Brands and Simply Good Foods as beneficiaries of that demand trend.
BofA describes Coca-Cola’s recent CAGNY presentation as "bottler friendly," which suggests confidence in the company’s alignment with its bottling partners and the way that could support the system’s long term economics.
Most recent commentary centers on higher targets and positive labels such as Buy or Overweight, so explicit bearish arguments are limited. This means potential risks around pricing, volumes or valuation are less discussed in the latest research set.

Coca-Cola’s Board approved the company’s 64th consecutive annual dividend increase, setting the quarterly dividend at US$0.53 per share, or US$2.12 per share on an annual basis, after returning US$8.8b in dividends to shareowners in 2025 and US$101.9b since January 1, 2010.
The company completed a share repurchase tranche under its February 21, 2019 buyback, retiring 83,051,256 shares, or 1.92% of the company, for US$5,158.45m. This included 1,447,290 shares for US$99.8m between September 27 and December 31, 2025.
Coca-Cola is preparing a proposed US$1b IPO of its Indian bottling arm, Hindustan Coca-Cola Beverages, with advisers targeting an implied valuation close to US$10b and aiming for a summer listing window.
The company plans to discontinue frozen products in the U.S. and Canada and was mentioned in coverage of Target’s move to require cereals without certified synthetic colors, which highlights how retailer ingredient policies may affect large food and beverage brands.
